01Company & Product Snapshot

RegMind is an AI-native compliance operating system for regulated financial institutions — banks, EMIs, and payment providers. It automates KYC/AML due diligence end-to-end through a deterministic, auditable pipeline, replacing manual, spreadsheet-and-Word compliance workflows with a structured, machine-enforced decision framework. The platform spans two surfaces: a client-facing onboarding portal and an internal compliance back-office.

Product
Compliance OS — intake → screening → risk scoring → document verification → memo generation → supervisor review → ongoing monitoring
Core differentiator
4-layer deterministic AI pipeline (rules → memo → validation → supervisor) with human approval gates and a cryptographic audit trail
Commercial status
One signed pilot (USD 60K ARR + USD 40K implementation); second pilot expected within ~6 months at comparable pricing
Platform depth
10 specialised AI agents · 35+ relational data domains · ~6,000 automated tests · validated AWS staging deployment

05Technology & IP Value

RegMind's core value is the encoded compliance domain expertise and the pipeline design that surrounds it — assets that cannot be bought off the shelf and take substantial specialist time to reconstruct. The commodity layers (infrastructure, standard application scaffolding, front-end) carry little independent value; a strategic buyer would retain the compliance IP and re-platform the rest.

Differentiated-IP replication floor: ≈ USD 1.0M. The estimated cost and 12–18 month timeline to independently rebuild the verification matrix, FATF-aligned risk model, deterministic memo/validation/supervisor pipeline, and jurisdiction-specific regulatory encoding — inclusive of compliance-consultant time — net of the engineering effort a buyer would invest to modernise the platform for scale.

09Key Risks & Mitigants

The principal risks at this stage are those typical of a pre-seed enterprise-software company, together with their mitigants and roadmap.

Early commercial stage
One signed pilot with billing not yet commenced; revenue is contracted rather than recognised.
Mitigant: customer is signed and billing begins H2 2026; a second pilot is in active pipeline; low-cost pilot structure de-risks conversion.
Single-jurisdiction concentration
Initial focus on the Mauritius FSC market, which is sizeable but finite.
Mitigant: the risk model and architecture are configurable and jurisdiction-agnostic by design; multi-jurisdiction expansion is on the near-term roadmap.
Screening-provider validation
AML screening currently integrates a primary provider; production validation of the secondary provider path is in progress.
Mitigant: a provider-abstraction layer is built to support multiple screening providers; production validation is an active, scoped workstream.
Certifications
SOC 2 / ISO 27001 not yet obtained, which some enterprise buyers require.
Mitigant: SOC 2 Type I is achievable within 3–6 months at modest cost and is a prioritised roadmap item.
Architecture scale-up
The platform is functional and well-tested, but portions of the codebase will benefit from modularisation to support horizontal scale and team growth.
Mitigant: ~6,000 automated tests provide a strong refactoring safety net; a service-layer decomposition is planned as part of the scale-up.
Team & execution
Continued delivery depends on a small core team, as is typical at this stage.
Mitigant: capital raised is intended in part to broaden the team across engineering and compliance functions.
