01 Valuation Methodology Selection

RegMind is, in substance, a pre-revenue pre-seed asset: one signed pilot that has not begun billing, one unsigned prospect, a single small jurisdiction, and no production customer. At this stage valuation is anchored on team, IP and market rather than on financials — no single method is decisive, and any method applied to a USD 60K base produces a wide range. A blended approach is used, with cost-to-replicate deliberately demoted to a floor reference rather than a value driver.

Method Applicable? Weight Rationale
Forward ARR multiple ✓ Primary (thin base) 35% Applied to contracted USD 60K and contingent USD 120K at an 8–15× pre-seed range. The base is small, so this anchors the lower half of the range rather than the headline.
Comparable early-stage rounds ✓ External anchor 30% Seed / pre-seed RegTech with IP and a design-partner pilot, stage-discounted for single-jurisdiction concentration and no live customer.
Cost-to-replicate (differentiated IP only) ✓ Floor reference 25% Limited to the hard-to-replicate compliance encoding and pipeline design, net of the refactoring liability a buyer must absorb. A floor, not a target.
Scorecard / Berkus ✓ Cross-check only 10% Sanity-check on team, product and market; limited precision given niche jurisdiction and unproven commercial traction.
DCF ✗ Not appropriate 0% No recognised revenue and one signed pilot. Multi-year projections cannot be constructed reliably.

Note on lines-of-code: codebase size (≈142K lines) is treated as scope evidence, not value. A large monolithic server.py is a liability to a technical acquirer, not asset mass — it cannot be counted as value on one page and as technical debt on another.

04 Differentiated-IP Replication Floor

Cost-to-replicate is included only as a floor, and only for the components an acquirer would actually retain. The commodity layers — generic CRUD/auth/RBAC, single-file frontends, basic infra — would be re-platformed by any buyer and add little to acquisition value, so they are excluded. What is genuinely hard to reconstruct is the encoded compliance domain expertise and the deterministic pipeline design.

09 Point Estimate with Scenario Range
Point estimate — blended methodology, all risk factors applied
USD 1.4M
Scenario range: USD 0.9M — 2.2M (reasoned spread, not a statistical interval)

Sits above the differentiated-IP floor (≈USD 1.0M) and within the pre-seed RegTech round bracket. The level at which a rational investor is not overpaying for unproven traction, nor a rational acquirer underpaying for the compliance IP.

Evidence gaps driving range width
  • Pilot 2 is unsigned. If it closes (~USD 60K), contingent ARR reaches USD 120K and the estimate moves toward USD 1.8–2.2M. If it slips or is lost, the estimate compresses toward USD 0.9–1.1M.
  • No revenue is yet recognised. Once Pilot 1 is live, billing, and collecting cleanly, contract-execution risk falls and the base estimate firms toward the upper half.
  • Team composition and equity structure are not evidenced. A solo or 2-person team carries a higher execution discount than a 4–6 person team with a dedicated compliance officer.
  • Regulatory validation is binary. A first FSC examination cycle completed using RegMind would revise the estimate to USD 2.0–2.8M with no other change.
Floor condition (USD 0.9M)

Pilot 2 does not sign; Pilot 1 alone, valued near the differentiated-IP floor net of the refactoring liability. Maximum jurisdiction-concentration discount applied. Below this, the asset is mispriced relative to its IP floor.

Ceiling condition (USD 2.2M)

Pilot 1 live and collecting, Pilot 2 signed, and either SOC 2 Type I underway or a second-jurisdiction configuration in progress. Beyond this requires a regulator-validated production customer (see §07).

Most actionable near-term levers: (1) get Pilot 1 live, billing, and stable — converting booked ARR into a live reference; (2) convert Pilot 2 from pipeline to signature. Together these move the estimate from ~USD 1.4M toward the USD 1.8–2.2M ceiling with no capital deployment.
