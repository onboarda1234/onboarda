# Evidence Export Procedure

**Use with:** `docs/pilot/COMPLIANCE_OFFICER_SOP.md`

An evidence export is a controlled snapshot for a defined purpose. It is not automatically a complete regulatory file, a guarantee of chain continuity, or proof that a decision was correct.

## 1. When to export evidence

Create an export when required by the approved operating policy, including:

- before final approval when the case route requires it;
- for Senior Compliance Officer review or an override;
- for internal QA or a pilot evidence bundle;
- for an approved audit or regulator request;
- when an incident or exception requires a preserved snapshot.

Do not create repeated exports merely to replace an unfavorable result. Preserve the original export and record why a later snapshot was created.

## 2. Pre-export checks

Before exporting, confirm:

- the application ID and reference are correct;
- the company and client are correct;
- the export actor is authorized;
- the snapshot purpose is recorded;
- the risk score, level, and provenance are current;
- the memo and validation/supervisor status are current;
- screening status and provider mode are visible and truthful;
- relevant documents and verification status are included or their absence is explicit;
- audit/activity entries and decision records are present where expected;
- RMI, enhanced requirements, periodic-review, and monitoring items are included or explicitly marked out of scope;
- unresolved blockers, limitations, and exceptions are recorded.

If the application identity or scope is uncertain, stop and resolve it before exporting.

## 3. Export procedure

1. Open the approved application record using its immutable application ID/ref.
2. Confirm the current status and review route.
3. Generate the approved evidence pack/export through the application workflow.
4. Verify the response, filename, application identity, timestamp, and reported record counts.
5. Open or inspect the export in a controlled location without sending it externally.
6. Confirm the export contains the expected risk, memo, screening, documents, audit, and decision sections for its purpose.
7. Record the export ID or filename, timestamp, actor, purpose, and storage location in the case record.
8. Record any missing section, partial result, provider limitation, fixture-assisted result, or chain-continuity limitation.
9. Escalate before relying on an incomplete or contradictory export.

For a regulator or external request, obtain management approval before release and use the approved secure transfer process. Do not email or upload an export to an unapproved destination.

## 4. What to verify in the export

- Correct application ID/ref and company identity.
- Snapshot timestamp and generating actor.
- Current risk level, score, dimensions, and provenance.
- Memo text, validation state, supervisor state, and approval state.
- Screening result, review status, provider mode, and any match interpretation.
- Documents, verification outcomes, metadata, and missing evidence.
- RMI and enhanced requirement state.
- Audit/activity trail, decision records, sign-offs, and relevant request IDs.
- Known exclusions or incomplete sections.

## 5. Export limitations

- An export is a snapshot and may be stale as soon as the underlying record changes.
- The officer must verify the timestamp and source application before relying on it.
- A partial export is not a complete regulatory file.
- Hash-chain or audit continuity evidence may have coverage limitations; do not imply global tamper-proof coverage when only a subset is present.
- A sandbox provider result, fixture-assisted result, or simulated result must be labelled truthfully and must not be presented as a live production clearance.
- An export does not replace the officer's review or the backend approval gate.
- Missing, redacted, or unavailable records must be listed as limitations, not silently omitted.

## 6. Storage and handling

- Store exports only in the approved restricted location.
- Apply the internal retention and access rules for compliance evidence.
- Avoid creating duplicate copies containing unnecessary PII.
- Do not place exports in personal drives, public links, chat channels, or unapproved ticket attachments.
- Share externally only after management approval and through the approved secure-transfer process.
- Do not alter an export after creation. If a correction or new snapshot is needed, create a new export and preserve the original.
- Report suspected disclosure, corruption, wrong-application export, or loss as an incident immediately.
