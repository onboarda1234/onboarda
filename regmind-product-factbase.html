01Executive Overview

RegMind is an AI-native compliance operating system for regulated financial institutions — banks, electronic money institutions (EMIs), and payment service providers. It automates KYC/AML customer due diligence end-to-end, from client intake through screening, risk scoring, document verification, compliance memo generation, supervisory review, and ongoing monitoring.

The platform is delivered as two branded surfaces: a client-facing onboarding portal ("Onboarda") and an internal compliance back-office ("RegMind"). It is currently deployed for a first paid pilot with an FSC-regulated institution in Mauritius, with a second pilot in the sales pipeline.

02Product Description

RegMind replaces manual, document- and spreadsheet-based compliance workflows with a structured, auditable, machine-enforced decision framework. It covers the full compliance lifecycle rather than a single function:

  • Client onboarding portal — applicant registration, company details capture, ownership/UBO structure, document upload, and real-time prescreening.
  • Screening & risk — sanctions / PEP / adverse-media screening, a configurable multi-dimensional risk-scoring model, and a screening review queue with disposition tracking.
  • Document verification — a multi-layer verification pipeline combining deterministic rule checks with AI interpretation across defined document types.
  • Compliance decisioning — structured compliance memo generation, automated validation, a supervisory contradiction-detection layer, and a multi-point human approval gate.
  • Ongoing lifecycle — enhanced due diligence (EDD), ongoing monitoring agents, materiality-tiered change management, and scheduled periodic reviews.
  • Audit & governance — a cryptographically hash-chained supervisor audit trail, a comprehensive action audit log, immutable PDF outputs, and role-based access control.
03How It Works — AI Pipeline & Agents

The core architectural feature is a 4-layer deterministic AI pipeline: rule engine → memo generation → validation engine → supervisor. AI is used for interpretation and narrative within the pipeline, but no probabilistic AI output can reach a final compliance decision without passing through deterministic validation and a human approval gate. This design directly addresses the primary objection regulators raise about AI in compliance.

LayerFunction
Rule engineDeterministic 5-dimension risk scoring with configurable weights, floor rules (e.g. sanctioned-jurisdiction forced high-risk), and escalation logic.
Memo generationTemplate-driven, rule-enforced compliance memo assembly; reproducible for a given input set.
Validation engineAutomated multi-point quality audit of the memo, producing a score and pass/fail verdict.
SupervisorContradiction detection across all prior stages; produces a consistency verdict and an "approvable" control flag.

The pipeline is supported by a catalogue of 10 specialised AI agents (of which 8–9 are active, one reserved for a future phase) covering identity/document checks, screening interpretation, corporate-structure analysis, memo/risk, and three ongoing-monitoring agents (periodic review, adverse media/PEP, behaviour/risk drift). Agents carry defined authority levels (authoritative vs. decision-support), and AI outputs are schema-validated.

06Intellectual Property & Defensibility

The company's principal proprietary assets are the encoded compliance domain expertise and the pipeline design:

  • Compliance encoding — the document verification matrix, FATF-aligned country/sector risk model, structured memo templates, validation ruleset, and supervisor contradiction framework.
  • Jurisdiction-specific regulatory logic — encoding aligned to the initial (Mauritius) regulatory environment, designed to be configurable for further jurisdictions.
  • Deterministic AI-control architecture — the design pattern of gating probabilistic AI behind deterministic checks and human approval.
  • Test corpus — ≈6,000 automated tests providing a regression safety net that a competitor rebuilding from scratch would need to reproduce.

09Market & Competitive Landscape

RegMind operates in KYC/AML compliance automation for regulated financial institutions. The initial served market is Mauritius FSC-regulated entities; the architecture is designed to extend to further jurisdictions (e.g. UK FCA, UAE DFSA, EU AMLD6).

  • Reference peers (larger scale, for orientation): Sumsub, ComplyAdvantage, Flagright and similar KYC/AML platforms.
  • Positioning: full-lifecycle compliance OS with deterministic AI controls, versus point tools (screening-only or verification-only) and versus AI-only tools that lack deterministic gating.

  • Pipeline conversion — Pilot 2 and further pipeline are not yet contracted.
